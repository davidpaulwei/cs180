1. Introduction

In the first part of this project, I morphed two face images using Affine Transformation. I obtained 100 correspondences for each of the two faces and computed their average coordinates. Triangulation was conducted on the correspondences and for each triangle, Affine Matrixes were generated to stretch the triangles from the original image to the Midway image. I used Cross Dissolve to bind the color. I furtherly generated a sequence of 51 morphed images using different Morph Weight to produce the morphing GIF. In the second part, I computed the mean face of 12 Brazilian faces, and stretched my face into the shape of the mean face. I also computed the mean face of 12 smiling Brazilian faces to add a smile on my grim portrait.

2. Correspondences and Triangulation

To morph the close-ups of Elon Musk and George Clooney by Martin Schoeller, I manually selected a total of 100 correspondences on each image. In this way, the two faces can be morphed by aligning the correspondences. I conducted Delaunay Triangulation on Elon's correspondences and adapted the triangulation on George's image. Each pixel in the two close-ups belongs to a triangle. The correspondences and triangulation of the images are shown in Figure 1.

3. Midway Face and the Morph Sequence

Midway Face is a generated morphed image where ideally the shape and color of the corresponding elements are the mean of that on the two faces. To generate the Midway Face, I computed the average coordinates of the correspondences on the two faces to represent the mean shape of each face elements, and the faces of Elon and George are aligned to the mean correspondences by conducting Affine Transformation on each triangle. The Midway face is the mean of the two aligned faces. It is shown in Figure 2.

To present a more vivid process of morphing, I generated a sequence containing 51 morphed images of Elon and George's closeups, where each image is assigned with a different Morph Weight. Morph Weight controls the extent of morphing and is within the range of [0, 1]. A morphed image with a weight of 0 is identical to the original Elon's closeup, with the same correspondence coordinates and the same color on the corresponding pixels. As the weight increases, the morphed correspondences become closer to that of George's image, and the color of the image dissolves to George's color. When the Morph Weight reaches 1, the output image becomes identical to the original George. This morphed image sequence is played at 50ms per frame to produce the morphing GIF shown in Figure 3.

4. Mean Face of Population

Using coordinates, we get to align and morph two faces into a midway face, or Mean Face. The same process can be adapted on a larger population. I aligned 12 Brazilian male faces from the FEI Face Database using 68 correspondences and then computed the mean. The 12 face closeups, the closeups after alignment, and the mean face are shown in Figure 4, 5, 6, respectively.

I aligned my face to the mean face to generate a Brazilian version of me, but still kept its color. This is similar to the morphing progress introduced in Section 3 only without the Cross-Dissolve. The output image is shown in Figure 7.

5. Make Me Smile! (Bells & Whistles)

In order to generate a smiling face using the original grim face of myself, I aligned my face to the mean face of a smiling Brazilian population. Figure 8, 9, 10 shows the smiling population, mean face, and my face before and after alignment, respectively.
